Overview

What is the Condition Monitoring System?

The Petrus CMS continuously measures the health of your most critical assets — motors, spindles, pumps, compressors, gearboxes, and presses — using vibration sensors, temperature probes, current clamps, and oil quality sensors.

Baseline health fingerprints are established for each machine. When deviations are detected beyond configured thresholds, ML models classify the anomaly and predict the likely failure mode — bearing wear, imbalance, misalignment, or overheating — before the machine fails.

This shifts your maintenance strategy from reactive to predictive — scheduling repairs during planned downtime windows instead of emergency stops that cascade across production.

Parameters Monitored:

  • ✓ Vibration amplitude (mm/s, g)
  • ✓ Bearing temperature (°C)
  • ✓ Motor current (A) & power (kW)
  • ✓ RPM and speed deviation
  • ✓ Oil pressure and viscosity
  • ✓ Acoustic emission for crack detection
  • ✓ Humidity and environmental factors
  • ✓ Run-time hours & duty cycles

How It Works

1
Sensor Installation — Wireless vibration + temperature sensors clamp onto motors, bearings, gearboxes. No machine downtime required for installation.
2
Baseline Learning — The system monitors normal operation for 1–2 weeks to establish machine-specific healthy fingerprints (vibration RMS, temperature ranges, current signatures).
3
Anomaly Detection — Continuous comparison of live readings vs. baseline. Threshold crossing or ML anomaly detection triggers the alert pipeline.
4
Alert & Work Order — Maintenance manager receives a structured alert with failure type, severity, and recommended action. A digital work order is created automatically.
5
Outcome Tracking — Post-repair closure is logged. MTBF and MTTR are updated. Spare parts consumed are deducted from stock automatically.
