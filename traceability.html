Overview

What is the Traceability System?

The Petrus Traceability System creates a full digital genealogy for every product — linking raw material supplier lot numbers, in-process measurements, assembly records, operator sign-offs, test results, and final dispatch details into one traceable DNA record per unit or batch.

When a customer complaint or recall occurs, you can trace backward in seconds: which machines processed the part, which operators handled it, what raw material batch it came from, and which other customers received parts from the same batch.

For OEM suppliers, this meets AIAG, IATF 16949, and PPAP documentation requirements. For pharma, it satisfies 21 CFR Part 11 and GMP batch record requirements.

Traceability covers:

  • ✓ Inbound raw material lots & supplier codes
  • ✓ WIP batch tracking across processes
  • ✓ Serial/batch number generation & labelling
  • ✓ Operator and machine linkage per operation
  • ✓ In-process measurements & test results
  • ✓ Sub-assembly genealogy (parent-child BOM)
  • ✓ Customer delivery linkage & dispatch records
  • ✓ Recall impact analysis (forward & backward)

Core Features

End-to-End Genealogy

Every finished product has a full digital pedigree — from supplier origin to customer destination — accessible in one click.

Batch & Serial Tracking

Auto-generate unique batch/serial numbers with configurable formats. Barcode or QR-code based labelling integrated directly with label printers.

Instant Recall Containment

Input a defective batch number and instantly see all products made from that batch — in stock, in transit, or at customer sites. Containment in minutes, not days.

Audit & Compliance Packs

Export complete product genealogy records in PDF or CSV for OEM PPAP submissions, pharma batch records, or customer audits.

Process Linkage (BOM-Based)

Link sub-assemblies and components to parent assemblies using your Bill of Materials. Track genealogy across multi-level production hierarchies.

Quality Linkage

Inspection results, NCRs, and test outcomes are linked directly to the traceable unit. Know quality history without searching through separate systems.
