Overview

What is the Energy Monitoring System?

The Petrus EMS sub-meters energy at the machine level — not just at the feeder panel. You can see exactly how much energy every motor, compressor, HVAC unit, or utility consumes in real time and historically.

By correlating energy consumption with production output, the system computes energy per unit (kWh/unit) for each machine and line. When a machine continues to draw power during an idle period, the system automatically flags it as waste.

For ESG-driven organisations, the EMS automatically calculates CO₂ equivalent emissions from energy data and generates BRSR, GRI, and CDP-compatible sustainability reports at the click of a button.

EMS measures:

  • ✓ Machine-wise kWh consumption
  • ✓ Voltage, current, power factor
  • ✓ Peak demand & MD charges
  • ✓ Energy per unit produced (kWh/unit)
  • ✓ Compressed air & water usage
  • ✓ Steam & nitrogen consumption
  • ✓ Solar & renewable contribution
  • ✓ CO₂ equivalent emissions (Scope 1 & 2)

Core Features

Machine-Level Sub-Metering

Smart energy meters are installed at each machine's distribution board. Live kWh data is streamed per machine every 15 seconds.

kWh/Unit Benchmarking

Correlate energy consumption with production count. Track energy intensity over time and compare across lines, shifts, and machines.

Peak Load Management

Monitor MD (Maximum Demand) in real time. Alert when approaching threshold and suggest load shifting to avoid expensive peak demand penalties.

Carbon Footprint Reporting

Auto-calculate Scope 1 and Scope 2 CO₂ emissions from energy data. Export board-ready ESG reports aligned with GRI, BRSR, and CDP standards.

Utility Monitoring

Extend beyond electricity — monitor compressed air, water, steam, chilled water, and nitrogen. Detect leaks via abnormal flow anomalies automatically.

Idle-Run & Waste Detection

Automatically identifies machines drawing power while in non-production state. Calculates waste in kWh and ₹/month so you can prioritise action.
